Alk Composite Membrane Hydrergy ZSM for Water Electrolysis

Product Overview

As the global energy transition and 'dual carbon' goals drive sustainable development, alkaline water electrolysis has emerged as a leading technology for green hydrogen production, commanding over 70% of the global water electrolysis market due to its cost-effectiveness and technological maturity. The composite membrane, a critical component of alkaline electrolyzers, directly impacts hydrogen production efficiency, energy consumption, and safety, making it a key factor in controlling green hydrogen production costs.

Hydrergy's advanced composite membrane, designed specifically for alkaline water electrolysis, offers exceptional low surface resistance, significantly reducing current loss and boosting electrolyzer efficiency while lowering energy costs per unit of green hydrogen. Its high bubble point pressure and low gas permeability effectively prevent gas crossover, mitigating safety risks and efficiency losses. Additionally, the membrane's robust adaptability to operating conditions extends its service life and reduces maintenance downtime, contributing to cost savings and enhanced efficiency in the green hydrogen industry.

Composite Membrane Characteristics

The Hydrergy series composite membrane, rigorously validated through nearly a thousand engineering tests, is crafted using an optimized blend of high-strength polymers and ceramic powders, combined with precision molding techniques. Its unique cross-section features micron-scale dense bubble pores, eliminating finger-like voids to ensure highly uniform pore size and precisely optimized porosity. This advanced structure delivers exceptional performance: low surface resistance for efficient operation, high bubble point pressure, and low gas permeability for superior gas-blocking safety. The Hydrergy membrane ensures outstanding electrolysis performance while providing robust and reliable safety for large-scale hydrogen production systems.
